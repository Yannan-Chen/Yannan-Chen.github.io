Who am I

I'm a PhD student at Columbia University's Biomedical Engineering department, working on large scale brain-wide mapping. My focus is on uniform labeling, high-throughput imaging, and comparative analysis of large brain samples using innovative techniques like aptamer-based staining, light sheet microscopy and unbiased phenotyping pipeline. With terabytes of neuroimaging data generated, I aim to understand the best principles for connecting observations to explanation.

What do I do

Mental and neurological disorders cause significant global disease burden, yet effective treatments are lacking. Whole brain mapping approaches offer a promising solution, but challenges remain in achieving uniform labeling and comparative data analysis. My work develops novel methods for uniform labeling, high-throughput imaging, and comparative analysis of large brain samples to understand the brain-wide impact of anti-depressant drug exposure.
